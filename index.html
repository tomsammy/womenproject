Meet Our Founder

Rev. Dr. Ijeoma Eteng Emeribe

"Our calling is to create sustainable safe spaces and write new economic realities for women across Africa through international career pathways."

Rev. Dr. Ijeoma Eteng Emeribe is an ordained minister, visionary social entrepreneur, and global humanitarian advocate. As the founder and president of Woman Africa International, she leads critical interventions that restore dignity to marginalized women and vulnerable groups, bridging direct relief with long-term global career paths.

NYC Patroness

Appointed Patroness of the Nigerian Youth Congress (NYC) in June 2022 due to her global leadership experience.

365 Days of Anti-GBV

Spearheading year-round SAFE shelter initiatives and policy changes to support domestic abuse survivors.

National Policy Voice

Public advocate calling for transparency, accountability, and qualified humanitarians in public welfare offices.

Family & Parenting

Promoting family love and proper parenting training as a primary vehicle to combat domestic violence.

How We Drive Global Impact

Woman Africa Foundation

Humanitarian Impact

Focused on identifying, sheltering, and rehabilitating vulnerable and displaced women affected by conflict. We provide skills development, trauma counseling, and our dedicated SAFE space for survivors of Gender-Based Violence.

Woman Africa Staffing

Economic Empowerment

The business and economic arm of our mission. It handles employment contracts, international workforce partnerships, and places fully rehabilitated women into safe, dignified, and sustainable jobs locally and abroad.
